Grandfathered vs. Non-Grandfathered Plans

ACA was signed into law March 23rd, 2010. If a health plan was written prior to this date and makes no material changes to their plan, they can remain grandfathered and not have to abide by all provisions of the health care law. If the plan was written after that date or makes a material change, they are non-grandfathered and will be subject to ALL provisions of ACA.

What is a material change?

  • Decrease in benefits of more than 15%: This would mean a deductible of $1000 could not be raised to more than $1150. A $20 copay could not be raised to more than $23. Since most carriers to not offer these options (usually offering a jump from $1000 to $1500, $20 to $30, etc.) in reality, almost any plan change where benefits are decreased results in a loss of grandfathered status.
  • Increase in employee’s premium contribution by more than 5% per year: If the employer increases the employee’s contribution of premium by more than 5% over last year’s contribution, they lose their grandfather status. This includes employees and dependents.

Lifetime Limits Removed (Effective September 23rd, 2010)

Previously companies used to be able to put a cap on the most they would pay on one person’s claims in a lifetime. Usually that amount ran around $5 million on most policies. That was removed on all plans, group and individual, grandfathered and non-grandfathered.

Annual Limits (Effective September 23rd, 2010)

Removes the annual dollar amount restrictions for certain services such as physical, speech and occupational therapy. The carriers can still limit on number of visits. This was removed for all plans except individual grandfathered plans.

Back To Top

Dependent Age (Effective September 23rd, 2010)

Increases the age a dependent can stay on their parent’s policy. They are able to stay on until their 26th birthday. There is no requirement of tax dependent status, marriage, residency, etc.

Child Pre-Ex (Effective September 23rd, 2010)

Applies to non-grandfathered group and individual policies only. Children under 19 have no 12 month waiting period on pre-existing conditions and are guaranteed issue for all plans, group and individual. BCBS of IL is the only company in the state writing this on a stand alone basis (without a parent on the policy, child only). The product is called Blue Pathways. Child can enroll at any time with a qualifying event, otherwise there is an annual enrollment period twice a year. This policy is direct written, no agent involvement.

Preventive Care (Effective September 23rd, 2010)

Provides coverage for defined preventative services at 100% coverage, no deductible or copays can be applied. This applies for non-grandfathered plans, group and individual. Preventative services must be classified as A or B services by the United States Preventive Task Force Service (USPTFS).

To see a list of preventive services, you can visit http://www.healthcare.gov/news/factsheets/2010/07/preventive-services-list.html

Examples of preventive services:

  • Annual Physicals
  • Immunizations
  • Mammogram (age limits apply)
  • Certain Conceptive Drugs
  • Colonoscopies (age limits apply)
  • Bone Density Scans (age limits apply)

In order to be covered under the policy as preventive, the doctor must code this as an annual wellness exam as opposed to a diagnostic code. On average, men get one physical per year covered, women get two.

Contraceptive Coverage (Effective August 1st, 2012)

Includes certain contraceptive drugs covered at 100% under preventive services for non-grandfathered policies, group and individual. Will be added to policies effective upon renewal. Each insurance company will provide a list of contraceptive drugs that are covered at 100%. Guide client to call membership to get an up to date list of drugs covered at 100%.

Minimum Loss Ratio (MLR) (Effective January 1, 2011)

Denotes that .80 cents on every dollar in premium paid for small group and individual policies and .85 cents on every dollar for large group must go towards paying claims, leaving the rest for administration costs. If an insurance company does not meet that MLR, they will issue rebate checks for the prior year. For example, rebates paid to groups in 2012 were for plan year 2011. Rebates are made to clients in the proportion of premium they paid in total.

Reporting on W-2 (Effective January 1st, 2011)

Originally the law stated that eventually all employers would have to report the amount of health benefits they paid for in a give year on their employee’s W-2, however, as the bill was implemented, this has been gradually changed and currently only applies to those companies issuing more than 250 W-2 in a given year.

Summary of Benefit and Coverage (Effective September 23rd, 2012)

The SBC is like a “nutrition label” for health insurance plans to make it easier for consumer to understand how their plan works. The law requires that all group and individual health plans distribute a SBC to members prior to enrollment, at renewal or when the group makes an off-renewal plan change. At renewal, the members must be given their SBC 10 days prior to their renewal.

In the group market, if the group makes a plan change off renewal within the same carrier, the group must provide the new SBC to the employees 60 days prior to the plan change. This is only required when making an off renewal plan change and does not apply when switching carriers. To comply with this rule, Humana is requesting all paperwork for off renewal plans changes 70 days in advance on the effective date with an attestation form confirming the SBC was distributed to the employees. BCBS has yet to define their policy.

In the individual market, the carriers will send the SBC direct to the consumer. In the group market, the carriers will send the SBC to the employer and the employer will have to distribute to the employees.

Employer Requirement to Provide Coverage (January 1st, 2014)

Requires employers with over 50 Full Time equivalent employees to provide “essential minimum coverage” to all full time employees (30 hours or more a week).

If an employer chooses not to offer coverage, they will face a fine of $2,000 for every full-time employee not counting the first 30. (For ie., if an employer have 70 full time employees, the fine would be (70-30) 2 $2,000) = $80,000.

Groups with under 50 FTE have will have no financial penalty if they do not provide coverage.

Full-Time Equivalent Equation

To determine how many full time employees a group have, the group must consider part time employees into the equation. Add the total number of hours worked by the part time employees in a month, divide by 120, and that will give you how many Full Time Equivalents the part time employees comprise. For example, if your part time employees worked an average of 35 employees that in total worked 3000 hours in a month, you would divide that by 120 to get 25 Full Time Equivalents. You would then add the 25 FTE to the number of full time employees you have to get your total FTE. If that number is over 50, the group is subject to the penalty.

Guaranteed Issue Individual Market (January 2014)

All non-grandfathered under 65 individual policies will be guaranteed issue with no pre-existing waiting period. Policies will no longer be able to have exclusion riders or be declined. Will most likely have an open enrollment period, with off open enrollment available for qualifying events only. Rating for these policies will be based on Community Rating, no health status. See below description.

Community Rating (January 2014)

Rating for non-grandfathered policies is to be limited to the following factors:

  • Age Rating (3:1 Ratio)
  • Family Size
  • Geographic Location
  • Tobacco Usage (1:1.5 Ratio)
  • No Health Rating

This type of rating system is called community rating, as the risk is spread among the community (ie. no health rating).

Individual Subsidy/Exchange (Effective Dates January 1st, 2014)

A subsidy will be available to help purchase insure to those individuals and families making less than 400% federal poverty level. This subsidy can only be claimed by purchasing insurance through the exchange. The subsidy would prevent premium from exceeding the following percentages of annual household income. Subsidy is based off premium for the silver plan.

  • 100%-133% FPL: 2% of income
  • 133%-150% FPL: 3%-4% of income
  • 150%-200% FPL: 4%-6.3% of income
  • 200%-250% FPL: 6.3%-8.05% of income
  • 250%-300% FPL: 8.05%-9.5% of income
  • 300%-400% FPL: 9.5% of income
  • Adjust for inflation in future years

Each state is responsible for setting up their own exchange website. If they wish not to set up their own, they can use the federal version of the exchange. Illinois has chosen to pursue a partnership exchange, where they use the federal exchange for the first year, transitioning into a state run exchange in year two.

The exchanges will be linked to the IRS to gather tax information to determined an individual’s eligibility for the subsidy. The insurance companies will get paid the subsidy amount from the Department of Treasury.

Reduced Out of Pocket Limits Subsidy (January 1st, 2014)

There is also a subsidy available for those people making less than 400% FPL that lowers their out of pocket limits. They must be enrolled in the silver plan in order to receive the out-of-pocket subsidy. This works similar to an HRA where HHS will reimburse the insurance company for any claims over the insured’s reduced limit. See the chart below for the limits.

  • 100%-200% FPL: one-third of the HSA limit
  • 200%-300% FPL: two-thirds of the HSA limit
  • 300%-400% FPL: two-thirds of the HSA limit

Individual Mandate (January 1st, 2014)

When filing taxes, each individual must prove that they have “essential minimum coverage” or face a penalty. Penalties are listed below.

  • 2014: The annual penalty will be $95 per adult and $47.50 per child up to a max of $285 per family or 1% of family income, whichever is greater.
  • 2015: The annual penalty will be $325 per adult and $162.50 per child up to a family maximum of $975 or 2% of family income, whichever is greater.
  • 2016: The annual penalty will be $695 per adult and $347.50 per child up to a family maximum of $2,085 or 2.5% of family income, whichever is greater.

Metal Plans

Plans will be tiered based on the Actuarial Value (AV) of the plan. They are classified by “metal” names. Actuarial Value is calculated as the percent of total average cost for covered benefits that a plan will cover. For example, if a plan has an AV of 70%, on average, a consumer would be responsible for 30% of covered benefits by way of deducible, coinsurance and copays. This is NOT the same as coinsurance. It is created so consumers can compare plans with similar benefits, even if deductible, coinsurance, and copay amounts are different.

  • Bronze Plan: AV of 60%
  • Silver Plan: AV of 70%
  • Gold Plan: AV of 80%
  • Platinum Plan: AV of 90%

More info can be found at http://www.healthcare.gov/news/factsheets/2012/11/ehb11202012a.html

Navigator vs. Agent

A Navigator is a salaried employee paid for by state grants to help guide consumers through the exchange. They cannot be paid directly or indirectly by an insurance company. Licensing and training requirement for navigators are yet to be defined. Agents will have access to sell in and out of the exchanges. A Commissioned agent cannot be a navigator.

Cadillac Tax (December 31st, 2017)

Establishes a 40% excise tax on insurers, but expected to be passed onto groups, that offer benefits that are too rich. Any plan that exceeds $10,200 in annual premium for an individual or $27,500 in annual premium for a family will be subject to the tax. The amount will adjust for inflation starting in 2019.

Small Business Tax Credit

As part of the ACA law, or Health Care reform, Small Businesses may be eligible for a tax credit of up to 35% from 2010-2013, and increasing to 50% in 2014 if certain criteria are met. The basic guidelines for the credit require a business to have less than 25 FTE (Full Time Equivalent) employees that each make less than an average of $50,000 a year.

There are currently phase outs for those employees making between $25,000 and $50,000 at a business with 10-25 employees. Those companies will not qualify for the entire 35%, and may not be eligible at all.

Please note when using this calculator that you visit the “?” key next to the FTE field to see what a Full Time Employee equivalent is. This is important to calculating the credit.
